Ratatouille

Description:

Ratatouille, a classic Provençal vegetable stew, became famous through the Pixar film "Ratatouille," where a rat named Remy dreams of being a chef. The dish features thinly sliced eggplant, zucchini, yellow squash, bell peppers, and tomatoes, arranged in a spiral pattern over a rich tomato sauce. Baked until tender and caramelized, the vegetables blend with the sauce for a vibrant, flavorful dish. Ratatouille is a feast for the eyes and palate, celebrated for its simplicity and fresh, seasonal ingredients, making it a perfect main or side dish.

Ingredients:

Main ingredients:

  • Eggplant - 1 medium, thinly sliced
  • Zucchini - 1 medium, thinly sliced
  • Yellow Squash - 1 medium, thinly sliced
  • Red Bell Pepper - 1, thinly sliced
  • Roma Tomatoes- 3-4, thinly sliced

Sauce ingredients:

  • Olive Oil - 2 tablespoons
  • Garlic - 3 cloves, minced
  • Onion - 1,finely chopped
  • Red Ball Pepper - 1, finely chopped (or 1 cup of crushed tomatoes)
  • Tomato Paste - 2 tablespoons
  • Vegetable Broth - 1/2 cup
  • Fresh Thyme - 1 teaspoon, chopped
  • Fresh Basil - 1 teaspoon, chopped
  • Salt and Pepper - to taste

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Sauce:
    1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
    2. Add minced garlic and chopped onion, and sauté until the onion is translucent.
    3. Add finely chopped red bell pepper and tomatoes, cook until they start to soften.
    4. Stir in the tomato paste, vegetable broth, thyme, basil, salt, and pepper. Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  2. Assemble the Ratatouille:
    1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. Spread the sauce in the bottom of a baking dish.
    3. Arrange the sliced vegetables (eggplant, zucchini, yellow squash, red and yellow bell peppers, tomatoes) in an alternating pattern on top of the sauce, creating a spiral or concentric circles.
  3. Bake:
    1. Drizzle a little olive oil over the top of the vegetables and season with salt, pepper, and a sprinkle of fresh herbs.
    2. Cover the dish with parchment paper or foil and bake for about 40 minutes.
    3. Remove the cover and bake for an additional 20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized.
Serve the ratatouille warm, as a main dish or a side, and enjoy the vibrant, comforting flavors of this classic Provençal vegetable stew that won the hearts of food critics and moviegoers alike!
